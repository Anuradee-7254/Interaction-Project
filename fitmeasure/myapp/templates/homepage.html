WHAT IS BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a numerical value derived from an individual's weight and height. It is used as a simple and quick screening tool to categorize people into different weight categories, which can indicate potential health risks associated with being underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese. While BMI is a useful tool and can give a general indication of whether an individual has a healthy body weight, it does have limitations. BMI does not directly measure body fat, and it does not take into account factors such as muscle mass, bone density, overall body composition, and racial and sex differences.

It's important to use BMI along with other health assessments and indicators for a complete evaluation of an individual's health. Consulting healthcare professionals for personalized advice is recommended.

BMI CATEGORIES

The value from the calculation of BMI is used to categorize what status the individual is in, and here are the different categories

Underweight

BMI less than 18.5, indicates being underweight; consult a provider for nutrition advice.

Normal Weight

BMI between 18.5 to 24.9, considered healthy; maintain with a balanced diet and regular exercise.

Overweight

BMI between 25 to 29.9, indicates being overweight; improve diet and increase physical activity.

Obesity

BMI between 30 to 34.9, indicates obesity; seek professional guidance for weight management.

Overobesity

BMI more than 35, indicates severe obesity; needs urgent medical consultation for a plan.

HOW TO MANAGE WEIGHT

To avoid the risk of various illnesses, we need to maintain our health and keep our weight within a suitable range. Effective weight management is actually not as difficult as it seems. It involves controlling your diet and engaging in regular exercise.

FOOD

Managing your diet isn’t about depriving yourself but about consuming a well-balanced diet with appropriate portions from all five food groups. For instance, women who are active in their work should aim for 1,200-1,600 calories a day, while men should target 1,800-2,000 calories, depending on their level of physical activity.

It's also important to focus on the quality of your food. Choose nutrient-rich foods like vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and high-quality proteins, and avoid those high in sugar, fats, and processed ingredients to reduce the risk of chronic diseases.

EXERCISE

Pairing a balanced diet with regular exercise is essential for maintaining weight and promoting overall health. Exercise helps burn excess calories, rebuild lost muscle mass, and supports long-term wellness. It also enhances cardiovascular health, mood, and energy levels, and can prevent chronic diseases like diabetes and heart disease.

A good exercise routine should include activity three times a week, with sessions lasting 20-60 minutes. Beginners, especially those who are significantly overweight, should start with lighter activities and consider seeking guidance from a healthcare professional or certified trainer for personalized advice.

DISEASES

Being overweight or obese can lead to various negative consequences.

Diabetes

Obesity can lead to diabetes by causing ineffective insulin production, resulting in high blood sugar levels.

Heart Disease

Excess fat can clog blood vessels, leading to reduced blood flow to the heart and potential heart damage.

Stroke

Fat buildup in brain blood vessels can block blood flow, leading to oxygen deprivation and possible brain damage.

High Blood Pressure

Being overweight often results in high blood pressure due to excessive fat in blood vessels, which can strain the heart.

Cancer

Obesity increases the risk of various cancers due to abnormal organ function from excess body fat.
